Notes from the research side of my work — life in a lab, papers in progress, and what I've learned from the long arc of a PhD.

## What this covers

- **Paper notes** — what I'm reading, what stuck, and what I'm trying to build on.
- **Experiments** — designs, dead ends, the messy middle that doesn't show up in the final draft.
- **Advisor dynamics** — managing up, scoping projects, navigating expectations.
- **The long arc** — milestones, qualifying exams, internship vs. research trade-offs, the year-3 slump.
- **Reproducibility** — environments, artifacts, what it actually takes to make systems research reproducible.

## Why I write about this

Most of the useful things I've learned in grad school don't show up in any paper. Some of them feel obvious in retrospect but cost a year to figure out. Writing them down is partly for me and partly for whoever's a year behind.
